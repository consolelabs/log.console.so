Console Log: June 2026

We shipped a leaner Mochi Pay, broke ICY's price feed for an embarrassing eleven minutes, and finally taught Tono to admit when it does not know. A solid month with one good scar. 🎮

tl;dr_

Four things shipped, one thing broke, and we learned that our cutest feature was also our slowest. We are heading into July lighter, with the treasury healthier than it has been all quarter.

shipped 🚀

  • Mochi Pay v2.4 trimmed the wallet-open flow from six taps to two. Money tooling should feel like a vending machine, not a form. 💰
  • Tono "I don't know" mode stops the bot from bluffing. An LLM that says nothing beats one that says something wrong.
  • ICY swap quotes now refresh on-chain every block instead of every thirty seconds. Tighter spreads, fewer surprised faces.
  • Cyber Neko sticker pack #3 hit Telegram. Small but sharp, and frankly very cute. 🔍
// shipped

Mochi Pay v2.4 is live for everyone, no waitlist, no gate. Accessible by default is the whole point. 🌐

thinking 🧠

The honest part. Our ICY price feed went stale for eleven minutes on the 18th because we trusted a single source and it quietly timed out. Nobody lost funds, but quotes drifted and we looked silly. Here is what the logs showed when we finally caught it:

neko-san@console · ~/icy 
$ icy-ops feed --check
WARN  primary oracle: last tick 11m02s ago
WARN  serving cached price (drift +1.8%)
$ icy-ops feed --failover backup
OK    backup oracle live, drift 0.0% ✓
// we broke it 💥

One oracle, no failover, no alert on staleness. Rookie. We added a second source and a staleness alarm the same night. We broke it, we said so, we fixed it. House style. 💥

The bigger shift: we are done treating "cute" and "fast" as a trade-off. The slow wallet flow taught us that the playful surface has to sit on a boring, reliable core. Use icy-ops feed --check before you trust a number, always.
