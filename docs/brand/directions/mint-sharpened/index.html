Console Log: June 2026

2026-06-30 / neko-san / #monthly #shipped

We shipped a leaner ICY top-up flow, broke (and fixed) Mochi Pay on Telegram, and spent a week arguing about whether a wallet should ever nag you. Some stuff rocked, some did not. Here is the scoop. 🐈‍⬛

TL;DR

The short version

June was a "sharpen what we have" month, not a "ship something big" month. The ICY treasury reconcile got simpler, Mochi got friendlier on mobile, and we killed one feature we were quietly proud of because nobody used it. We are okay with that.

Shipped

What went out the door 🚀

  • ICY top-up, one fewer step. The swap confirm and the backing check are now a single screen. Why it matters: fewer taps means fewer ways to fat-finger a treasury move.
  • Mochi Pay on Telegram, unbroken. A silent 409 busy-loop was eating replies. Why it matters: the wallet that does not answer is not a wallet.
  • Tono reads threads now. Our Discord LLM bot finally follows a reply chain. Why it matters: context in, less nonsense out.

Heads up

The old /icy adjust Discord command is gone. Treasury moves are treasurer-only now, run straight against the ledger. Same safety, less surface.

Thinking

What we are chewing on 🔍

The feature we cut was a "gentle reminder to rebalance". It tested fine and shipped clean, and then we watched the numbers: almost nobody opened it, and the few who did found it annoying. A wallet that nags is a wallet you mute. So we pulled it.

The lesson we keep relearning: small but sharp is a discipline, not a slogan. Every screen we add is a screen we have to defend. June's win was not a launch, it was a deletion. More on the reasoning in the playbook.

, neko-san
