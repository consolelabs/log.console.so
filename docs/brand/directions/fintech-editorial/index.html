Shipped

What went out the door 🚢

  • Mochi Pay: swap-from-chat. You can now quote and execute a swap without leaving the Discord thread. Why it matters: fewer steps between intent and settlement.
  • Tono context windows. The LLM bot now remembers a channel's last few turns. Why it matters: it stops asking what you already told it.
  • ICY snapshot endpoint. A read-only icy-ops snapshot that prints circulating supply against backing. Why it matters: the treasury reconciles in public.

✅ Shipped

Mochi Pay swap-from-chat is live on mainnet for the Console Labs server. Roll-out to partner servers is gated behind one more week of watching the fee math.

Thinking

The part where we narrate ourselves 🧭

We keep circling one question: how much of a wallet should live inside a chat app? The swap-from-chat work pushed us toward an answer. Money tooling should meet people where they already are, but it has to carry its own seatbelts. So we spent half the month not on features but on the quote-confirm-settle handshake, the boring safety glue. Read the design note for the full reasoning.

Small but sharp is not a size, it is a refusal. We refuse to ship the tool that does nine things adequately.

, neko-san

One thing we tried that did not work

We prototyped a gamified streak system for daily swaps. The idea was cute. The data was not: it nudged people toward churn-y micro-transactions that helped nobody and looked like a slot machine. We killed it after a week.

🧪 Heads up

The swap-streak experiment flopped. We are leaving the writeup public because the house style is honesty, and because the failure is more useful than the pitch was.

# what the streak nudge actually did
median swap size  ↓ 38%
swaps per user    ↑ 5.1x
net fees retained ~ flat   # churn, not value

Next

What we are chasing 🎯

Hardening the swap handshake for partner servers, a first pass at Tono summarizing a channel on demand, and a long-overdue cleanup of the ICY reconcile job. Light intentions, not a roadmap promise.
